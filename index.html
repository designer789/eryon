FAQ

1. What is Eryon?

Eryon is a Web3-native smart ring that acts as a decentralized physical infrastructure (DePIN) node. It enables users to contribute data, verify identity, make contactless payments, and interact with dApps—all through a compact wearable device.

2. How can I earn $EON tokens?

You earn $EON by sharing location, health, or environmental data, verifying network data, participating in edge computing tasks, and using the ring for decentralized payments. All actions are rewarded based on real-world contribution.

3. Is the Eryon ring a crypto hardware wallet?

Yes. The ring securely stores your cryptographic keys and uses biometric signals (like heartbeat or touch) for authentication. It lets you sign transactions, log in to dApps, or access assets—without exposing private keys.

4. What kind of data does the ring collect?

Eryon collects geo-location, temperature, humidity, air quality, heart rate, movement, and touch data. All data is anonymized and encrypted before being uploaded to the network, and users are rewarded based on data quality and frequency.

5. How does Eryon protect my privacy?

Eryon uses local AI inference and end-to-end encryption to ensure your data stays private and secure. You control what is shared—and you get rewarded for it.

6. How is the ring powered and connected?

The ring is ultra-lightweight (<5g), with a battery life of 7–14 days. It connects to your smartphone via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, enabling data sync, on-chain interactions, and app integration without compromising performance.
